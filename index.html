General Information

We believe that students should see the relevance of education to their lives and futures. By tying global challenges to school subjects, Omega 2 motivates young scholars to:

  • Pursue knowledge with greater purpose
  • Recognize the value of diverse professions
  • Build resilience and adaptability for the future

The Omega 2 Project was founded by Mark Shirokii, Youth Representative of Latvia at the United Nations High-Level Political Forum 2025. Omega 2 was created to address a growing challenge: many students feel that school subjects are disconnected from their future aspirations, leading to lost motivation and missed opportunities.

Omega 2 bridges this gap by linking academic knowledge with real-world professions. Through competitions, interviews with professionals, and hands-on challenges, students gain a clearer vision of how their interests connect to future careers.

The Omega 2 Olympiad is the core activity of the project — an annual competition where students tackle challenges inspired by the world’s most pressing problems.

Topics covered

  • Extreme Weather & the Climate Crisis
  • Biodiversity Loss and Ecosystem Collapse
  • Natural Resource Shortages
  • Armed Conflicts and Verification Technologies
  • AI Safety and Emerging Technologies
  • Health and Well-Being
  • Plastic Pollution
  • Air Pollution
  • Ocean Acidification
  • Soil Degradation and Food Security

  • Develop teamwork, creativity, and problem-solving skills
  • Round 1 (Online – 26.09.2025): Gamified problem-solving tasks combining math, physics, chemistry, biology, economy and social sciences. Students work through interactive scenarios such as hurricane simulations, climate modeling, or ecosystem restoration puzzles.
  • Round 2 (Final – 22.11.2025): Selected finalists compete in a live event featuring deeper problem-solving, teamwork, and presentations.

All tasks are designed to be accessible to high school students, with provided background theory and step-by-step guidance — no prior advanced knowledge required.
